Work Experience

Graduate Research Assistant

August 2020 - Present

Syracuse University Metadata Lab

I was selected to work with program director Dr. Jian Qin and her Metadata Lab as a part of the Wilhelm Library Leadership Scholarship. I have been working primarily on a project informally titled Linked Archives, which is about representing special collections metadata as linked data, enriching it with existing metadata from Wikidata, and importing the information into a local instance of Wikibase. I have been combining my skills with computer science and library science to take charge of this project's progress.

Librarian

March 2018 - May 2020

Ithaca College Books Thru Bars

I joined ICBTB in my first semester of college to help the club mail books to incarcerated people all across the country. At the end of my sophomore year, I took over the position of Librarian, which involved managing the organization's collection of books, including logging in donations, logging out mailed books, and developing a genre label system to keep the books better organized. In my senior year, I also worked with a fellow student to develop a formal database and website for the organization to both showcase our work and have an information system that is much more accessible to the executive board members.

Office Assistant

June 2015 - August 2020 (Summers)

The Children's Movement Center

Throughout my years working school breaks at the CMC, I got involved with a wide variety of projects, from editing marketing videos to tying a new net in the play center to completing billing in Quickbooks to keeping the business website updated. I also worked on the employee handbook, promotional flyers, brochures, and social media posts, and handled confidential client and employee information. My favorite project was the creation of a smart attendance spreadsheet in Google Sheets, which allowed management to input program registration data and automatically generate a variety of information sheets.

Undergraduate Research Assistant

January 2018 - August 2018

Ithaca College Department of Computer Science

In my sophomore year of college, I was invited to participate in a reserach project called Play | Write, which was about the use of virtual reality for creative writing. I was part of a small team that built a prototype VR system using Unity, the HTC Vive, and Leap Motion VR. Our virtual world gave users the ability to control their environment, create floating idea boards, and virtually track their desk and chair, and access their desktop from inside the system.
